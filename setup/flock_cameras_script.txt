Flock Cameras Explained (Simple + Informative)

Flock Safety cameras are automated license plate readers placed around
streets, neighborhoods, and intersections.

Their job is simple: Capture every car that passes and turn it into
data.

What They Collect: - License plate number - Car color, make, and model -
Exact time and location

This information is uploaded to a searchable system.

How Many Are Out There: - Used in 3,000+ communities across the U.S. -
Estimated tens of thousands of cameras nationwide - Growing rapidly
every year

How They’re Paid For: - Around $2,000–$3,000 per camera per year - Paid
by police departments, government grants, HOAs, and businesses

What They’re Used For: - Finding stolen vehicles - Tracking suspects -
Solving crimes - Monitoring neighborhood traffic

The Important Part: One camera doesn’t mean much, but multiple cameras
create a timeline of your movements.

Over time, this can reveal: - Where you live - Where you work - Your
daily routine

Things Most People Don’t Know: - Data is typically stored for about 30
days - Your car becomes your identity - False reads can happen - Data
raises privacy concerns

Closing Line: Every time you drive past one of these, you’re not just
being seen — you’re being logged, stored, and turned into data.
